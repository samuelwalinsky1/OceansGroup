Food is important to staying alive anywhere on the earth, and this doesnt change by and in the ocean!
The Indian Ocean has food sources for all of the animals living in it, but humans fish and harvest from the ocean too. Here is a source talking more about the natural resources we harvest from this ocean.




The Food Web of the Indian Ocean
Trying to track every relationship between what plants and animals eat is nearly impossible, but we can categorize them! On the lowest level there are producers like algae, seagrass, seaweed, and plankton. On the next level there is eel, dugong, clown anenome fish, and sea otters. At the top level there are killer whales, whale sharks, giant squid, sea turtles, and bottlenose dolphins.
